Background

The COVID-19 pandemic has sent shockwaves through cities globally, triggering multifaceted disruptions across every facet of urban life. One of the most conspicuous consequences has been the significant downturn in public transportation ridership, with commuters worldwide reevaluating their travel habits in light of health and safety concerns.
In response to this paradigm shift, our study embarks on a comprehensive examination of the impact of COVID-19 on MRT travel behavior across various planning areas in Singapore.

Objective

Analyze the epidemic's impact on Singapore's subway usage, focusing on passenger flow changes, demographic shifts, and geographical factors before and after the epidemic.

Examine cognitive biases and social psychological influences on subway safety perceptions and usage behavior during the epidemic, utilizing survey data and observations.
